## What ChinaCheck Does

ChinaCheck is built for European hospitality operators who want a sharper picture of how ready they are to serve guests from China. It combines local demand signals with practical readiness checks so teams can compare market potential and spot the areas where action is likely to have the biggest impact.

The tool is a fit for owners, managers, and commercial teams who need a fast way to review channel coverage, payment support, language accessibility, and digital touchpoints. By organizing those inputs into a structured readiness view, ChinaCheck makes it easier to focus on the operational changes that may strengthen appeal to Chinese travelers.

## Key Capabilities

- Measures Chinese traveler demand by location
- Evaluates readiness for Chinese guests across core service areas
- Surfaces gaps in channel coverage, payment support, language, and digital accessibility
- Estimates possible revenue upside from improving China readiness
- Helps with hospitality planning and market prioritization
- Supports teams in spotting operational weaknesses before expanding outreach
- Delivers readiness insights through a web-based workflow
- Useful for commercial, operations, and guest-experience planning

## Installation

ChinaCheck is provided as a web project. To use a local copy, clone the repository and open it in your browser, or serve it with a basic static server.

Clone the repository:
- `git clone https://github.com/benwoodrfa6876/chinacheck-hospitality-hub.git
- `cd chinacheck`

Start a local server if needed:
- `python -m http.server 8000`

Then open the app in your browser at `http://localhost:8000`.

## How to Use It

1. Launch the web app in a browser.
2. Inspect the location-based demand view.
3. Review readiness signals for channel, payment, language, and digital accessibility.
4. Compare the listed gaps with your current guest-facing setup.
5. Use the revenue estimate to decide which improvements matter most.

For a practical workflow, begin with the locations you already serve, then broaden the review to nearby markets where Chinese traveler demand may support additional preparation.
